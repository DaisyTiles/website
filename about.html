About

Our Team Daisy Tiles

I'm Nicolas Quijano, a third year Electrical and Computer Engineering student at CU Boulder. I've always had a passion for teaching people programming and hardware. When teaching younger students, I saw a large first step to using an embedded systems. Not only does it often require learning the basics of programming, but also either OOP or advanced SDKs, alongside with the basics of circuitry.

This is why I created Daisy Tiles. It gets rid of the big first step by exposing a much simple, block-based programming system that relies on programming-by-device as opposed to programming-by-port. This abstracts concepts such as GPIO configurations and the concept of analog vs digital devices. Daisy tiles also features a simple plug-and-play tiled system, getting rid of any worries about resistors and messy wires.

As a user gets more advanced, the IDE also exposes more complex programming concepts. This includes things like programming-by-port, bit manipulation, and more.
