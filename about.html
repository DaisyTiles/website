Daisy Tiles

We created Daisy Tiles because we saw how big the first step to learning an embedded system is. It gets rid of that by exposing a much simple, block-based programming system that relies on programming-by-device as opposed to programming-by-port. This abstracts concepts such as GPIO configurations and the concept of analog vs digital devices. Daisy tiles also features a simple plug-and-play tiled system, getting rid of any worries about resistors and messy wires.

As a user gets more advanced, the IDE also exposes more complex programming concepts. This includes things like programming-by-port, bit manipulation, and more.
